The Investment Game (Brief)

You have the opportunity to buy-in to this game next week with $30,000. Your job is to analyze the potential outcomes of the game and communicate why you should or should not buy-in.

Each year after buy-in you flip a fair coin:

  • Heads: increase your account balance by 50%
  • Tails: decrease your account balance by 40%

You play annually until age 75. Your mission is to analyze outcomes and communicate insights clearly.

1) Expected Value After 1 Flip

Explain whether the expected value of your account balance after one flip is greater than, equal to, or less than $30,000. What is the gain in expected value as a percentage of your buy-in? Based on this simple analysis, state whether you would recommend buying into the game and why.

1) Solution:

After one flip, the expected account balance is $31,500, which represents a 5% increase over the initial $30,000 investment. This is calculated by taking the average of the two possible outcomes: a 50% gain (to $45,000) and a 40% loss (to $18,000), each with equal probability.

This positive expected value indicates that, on average, the game is favorable and generates a return above the initial investment. In other words, if this game were played many times, the average outcome would result in a gain of $1,500 per play.

Based solely on this simple expected value analysis, I would recommend buying into the game because it offers a positive return. However, this conclusion does not yet account for the variability and risk of outcomes, which will be explored in later sections.

6) The Kelly Criterion

Explain what the Kelly Criterion is and how it relates to the modified strategy from Section 5. Does the Kelly Criterion support your recommendation?

6) Solution:

The Kelly Criterion is a method used to decide how much of your money you should risk in a bet or investment in order to grow your wealth over time while avoiding large losses. Instead of betting everything, it tells you to only risk a certain percentage that gives the best long-term results.

In this game, even though the expected value is positive, the outcomes are very risky when the full amount is bet each time. The Kelly Criterion suggests that only a portion of wealth should be invested in each round, rather than the entire balance.

Based on the probabilities and payoffs of this game, the optimal fraction to bet is roughly around 20–25%, which is very close to the modified strategy analyzed in Part 5.

The simulation results support this idea. When only 25% of wealth is bet each year, the outcomes are more stable, the probability of making a profit is much higher, and the risk of large losses is reduced.

Therefore, the Kelly Criterion supports the recommendation to use the 25% strategy instead of betting the full amount, as it provides a better balance between growth and risk.
